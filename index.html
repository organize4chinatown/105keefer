570 COLUMBIA IS STILL 105 KEEFER — AND WE STILL DON’T WANT IT

you can change the name, but you can’t change our history!

For over ten years, 105 Keefer has been at the center of a fight for Chinatown’s future. Developer Beedie Living wants to turn this site into luxury condos. Our community has said again and again: we need affordable homes for people and not condos for investors. This fight is not just about one building. It’s about cultivating a thriving community in Chinatown and the Downtown Eastside (DTES), where low-income seniors can age in place and where unhoused residents, workers, and youth can find homes. Our neighbourhood is not to be carved up for profit and speculation.

What’s Happening Now

Beedie has rebranded 105 Keefer as 570 Columbia in an attempt to erase the history of resistance tied to this site. But it’s the same project — and they are getting bolder:

  • More condos, increased density, and no social housing
  • Setback and height limits ignored
  • The building overshadows and encroaches on Memorial Square
  • A community service space shoved into a back alley, small and inaccessible

Our History of Resistance

  1. 2017

    Fuelled by greed, Beedie applied for rezoning to increase the size of their condo development–we fought against their applications every time. After four failed rezoning attempts, Beedie tried to bypass the City Council by going straight to the Development Permit Board (DPB). They stripped out social housing entirely. Over 200 people spoke against the project. For the first time in decades, the DPB said “no”. This was a historic win for Chinatown and for the housing justice movement as a whole in Vancouver.

  2. 2017–2022

    Beedie spent millions of dollars going through the legal process to overturn the decision. Their money took them all the way to the BC Supreme Court, who ordered the DPB to rehear the case.

  3. 2023

    The project came back. Over 500 seniors, tenants, and residents from Chinatown and the DTES filled a community town hall and voted “no” to condos and unanimously “yes” to 100% social housing. For a month, people rallied outside City Hall. Despite hearing numerous speeches in opposition, the DPB granted Beedie a permit “with conditions.” However, the fight reinvigorated residents and organizers to build a more sustained movement.

Myths vs. Truths

Myth: Condos will “revitalize” Chinatown.

Truth: Condos push out the very people who make this community vibrant by raising rents and property taxes. Real revitalization means housing and services that serve our community.

Myth: Any new housing is good.

Truth: If it isn’t affordable, it exacerbates the deeply unaffordable housing crisis. Luxury condos often sit empty as investments and people who can’t afford to live there are discriminated against. We need community-serving affordable housing — real homes for people, not profit.

Myth: Chinatown has no “residential base”.

Truth: What they mean is Chinatown doesn’t have affluent residents. That claim erases the thousands of people who already live here and depend on this neighbourhood every day.

Why This Matters

  • Displaces seniors who rely on SROs, supportive, and senior housing
  • Forces out culturally relevant and affordable shops and services in Chinatown
  • Increases surveillance and discrimination against residents, especially low-income and unhoused people

These harms are already happening—and will worsen if we don’t act now.
